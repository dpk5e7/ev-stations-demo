The Limit field only is concerned with the StationsNearbyRoute API.
'all' works just fine. The problem comes in when you insert a limit. It grabs the stations near the start of the lineString which is where one would expect the electric vehicle to be fully charged anyway and you wouldn't need to locate a charging station. For example, if you start your route in Denver, CO, all 200 EV Charging Stations will be in Denver when you wouldn't need a recharge until you get near Limon if you're driving east on I-70.
It'd be much more helpful to still find charging stations throughout the route even when there's a limit imposed on the number of stations returned. Ideally, it'd be useful if stations were identified near where the EV should recharge.
